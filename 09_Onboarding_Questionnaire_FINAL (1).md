# How to use this document

> **•** Every business completes the 23 universal questions.
>
> **•** After that, the business answers only the section for its
> selected industry.
>
> **•** Most industries use 3 follow-up questions. Window Cleaning uses
> 2.
>
> **•** All questions are open text. If something does not apply, the
> owner can leave it blank.

# Part I - Universal Questions

These questions apply to every business and give the AI the core rules
for messaging, quoting, scheduling, escalation, customer expectations,
and post-job follow-up.

### **1. 1. Business identity and sign-off**

> What is your business name, your name and role, and what name should
> the AI sign off with in messages?

### **2. 2. Urgent alerts and escalation contacts**

> What is the best phone number and email to reach you for urgent
> alerts? If you have a secondary contact or if different people handle
> different things, list them all.

### **3. 3. Business hours and closures**

> What are your normal business hours for every day of the week, and any
> holidays or seasonal closures?

### **4. 4. Services offered**

> List every service your business offers, including any recurring or
> maintenance services.

### **5. 5. Work you do not do**

> What work do you NOT do? Include services you get asked about but
> don\'t offer, property types you won\'t work on, and anything that\'s
> out of scope for your business.

### **6. 6. Service area**

> What is your service area? List the cities, zip codes, counties, or
> regions you cover and anywhere you specifically do NOT go.

### **7. 7. Owner-approval jobs**

> What types of jobs require your personal approval before the AI can
> book or confirm? Include any rules about additional work discovered
> during a job.

### **8. 8. Fixed prices and fees**

> List every fixed price, starting price, package price, trip fee,
> diagnostic fee, after-hours fee, disposal fee, emergency fee, minimum
> charge, and any other fee or charge the AI is allowed to share with
> customers.

### **9. 9. Pricing logic for custom work**

> For services without fixed prices, describe how your pricing works.
> Can the AI give customers rough estimates when you provide the parts
> cost through the dashboard, or should all custom pricing go strictly
> through you?

### **10. 10. Quote requirements**

> What information do you need from a customer before you can give them
> a quote? Walk through everything.

### **11. 11. Payment methods and terms**

> What payment methods do you accept, and when is payment due?

### **12. 12. Appointment types and booking rules**

> What types of appointments do you offer, how long does each one take,
> and how far in advance can customers book? Is same-day booking
> allowed?

### **13. 13. Customer prep before arrival**

> What does a customer need to have ready before your team arrives?

### **14. 14. Cancellation and rescheduling policy**

> What is your cancellation and rescheduling policy?

### **15. 15. AI voice and languages**

> Describe how you want the AI to sound when talking to your customers.
> What personality and tone should it have? If you serve customers in
> languages other than English, list those languages here and the AI
> will respond in the customer's language when possible. If you only
> serve English-speaking customers, just say English.

### **16. 16. Always say / never say**

> Is there anything the AI should always say or always mention? Anything
> it should never say, never promise, or never discuss?

### **17. 17. Emergencies and after-hours**

> What counts as an emergency in your line of work, what should the AI
> tell customers during an emergency, and how should things work
> differently outside business hours?

### **18. 18. Common customer questions**

> What are the most common questions your customers ask? List as many as
> you can with how you want them answered.

### **19. 19. Typical customer process**

> Describe your typical process from first customer contact to job
> completion. Walk through every step.

### **20. 20. Important business details**

> What should customers know about your business that most people don\'t
> think to ask?

### **21. 21. Reviews and post-job instructions**

> What is your Google review link, what specific post-job message or
> instructions should the AI send after a job is done, and what phone
> number should customers use if they have future questions or issues
> about that completed job?

### **22. 22. Warranty and callback policy**

> Do you offer any warranty, workmanship guarantee, callback policy, or
> satisfaction guarantee? If it\'s not formally written, describe how
> you normally handle it when a customer has an issue after a job.

### **23. 23. Final context and customer philosophy**

> Is there anything else the AI should know about your business? And if
> you could tell a new receptionist one thing on their first day about
> how you want customers treated, what would it be?

# Implementation Notes

> **•** Recommended onboarding flow: choose industry -\> answer 23
> universal questions -\> answer the matching industry section -\>
> review summary -\> confirm and save.
>
> **•** Universal question count: 23.
>
> **•** Industry question count: usually 3, except Window Cleaning which
> uses 2.
>
> **•** Actual total per business: 26 questions for most industries, 25
> for Window Cleaning.
>
> **•** Universal questions define general business rules. Industry
> questions add trade-specific intake, safety, prep, pricing, and
> expectation-setting context.
>
> **•** During onboarding, the system must also capture the business
> timezone (auto-detected from browser or manually selected). This is
> stored on the businesses table and used for all quiet-hours,
> business-hours, and timing calculations. It is not a questionnaire
> question — it is captured automatically or as a setup field.
>
> **•** The owner also creates a join code during onboarding. This is
> the code admins will use to join the business.
>
> **•** All questions must be answered. "N/A" is accepted as a valid
> answer meaning not applicable. Onboarding is blocked until all
> questions for the selected industry are complete.
>
> **•** The owner can go back and edit any answer during onboarding
> before final submit. All answers are also editable in settings after
> onboarding.
>
> **•** Each answer is stored BOTH as raw text and as structured data.
> Raw text preserves what the owner typed. Structured data (parsed into
> lists, schedule objects, service records, etc.) is what the AI reads
> at runtime. If parsing fails, the AI falls back to raw text.
>
> **•** Settings edits update both raw text and structured fields
> immediately. AI behavior changes immediately on save.
